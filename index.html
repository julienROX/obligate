How it reads. Per reserve, Borrow APR = IRM(utilization) and Supply APY = Borrow APR × utilization × (1 − take rate). A reserve clears at the highest utilization where borrower demand (only borrowers whose max borrow APR ≥ the reserve rate) still covers the borrowed amount. Direct suppliers join while the reserve Supply APY ≥ their min APY (committed always in). Vault liquidity is spread over borrowable reserves by weight, capped by each deposit cap; undeployed capital dilutes the blended vault APY. Turn override on to pin a reserve's utilization instead of solving it.

Design a reserve the way a curator would, and watch how each parameter changes the experience for a lender (who deposits to earn) and a looper (who deposits collateral and borrows to lever up). Editable curator knobs are at the bottom; the user-facing impact is at the top.

Reading the impact. LTV caps how much a looper can borrow against the collateral → max leverage = 1 / (1 − LTV). Liquidation threshold (LLTV) is the buffer before liquidation; the gap LLTV − LTV sets how far the collateral can fall before a max-levered looper is liquidated. Borrow factor inflates the counted value of the borrowed asset, shrinking effective leverage for risky debt. The IRM sets borrow APR vs utilization, which (× utilization × (1 − take rate)) becomes the lender's Supply APY. Caps bound how much can be deposited/borrowed. Liquidation bonus is the discount a liquidator gets — the looper's penalty if liquidated.
