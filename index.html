How it reads. Per borrowable reserve, Borrow APR = IRM(utilization) and Supply APY = Borrow APR × utilization × (1 − take rate). A reserve clears at the highest utilization (bounded by its utilization cap) where borrower demand still covers the borrowed amount. Vault liquidity spreads over borrowable reserves by weight, capped by each deposit cap; undeployed capital dilutes the blended vault APY. Turn override on to pin a reserve's utilization.

Reading the impact. LTV caps how much a looper can borrow against the collateral → max leverage = 1 / (1 − LTV). Liq. threshold (LLTV) is the buffer before liquidation; the gap LLTV − LTV sets how far the collateral can fall before a max-levered looper is liquidated. Borrow factor inflates the counted value of the borrowed asset, shrinking effective leverage. The IRM sets borrow APR vs utilization → Supply APY. Utilization cap blocks new borrows above a threshold. Liq. bonus is the liquidator's discount — the looper's penalty.
